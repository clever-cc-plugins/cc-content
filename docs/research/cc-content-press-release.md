# Research: Press Releases

Gathered via `WebSearch` on 2026-07-20 for `cc-content-press-release`. Covers the six
research areas from `_shared/content-skill-research-brief.md`. Hard numbers are cited
inline; anything without a source is marked `⚠ KNOWLEDGE-BASED`.

## 1. Universal Structure

- **Overall structure — inverted pyramid:** most important information first, supporting
  details in descending order of importance, so the core message survives even if an
  editor trims from the bottom. [eReleases, AP Style for Press Releases](https://www.ereleases.com/press-release-sample/ap-style-for-press-releases-the-ultimate-guide/)
- **Headline:** title case, under 100 characters (this length attracts the most views),
  no ending punctuation unless a question mark or exclamation point is grammatically
  necessary (in practice, use neither). [eReleases, ibid.]
- **Dateline:** city name in ALL CAPS opens the first paragraph, followed by a long
  dash with a single space on each side, then the body text begins (`CITY — Body text
begins here`). Add the state abbreviation if the city is not on AP's standalone-city
  list (e.g., `PORTLAND, Ore. —`, not `PORTLAND —`). [eReleases, ibid.]
- **Lead / opening paragraph:** must answer the 5 Ws (who, what, when, where, why)
  within 50-75 words. [eReleases, ibid.]
- **AP style numeral/date/title conventions (2026):** spell out numbers one through
  nine, use figures for 10+; always use digits for percentages and money; abbreviate
  months with 6+ letters when paired with a specific date (e.g., "Jan. 12, 2026"),
  spell out shorter months; capitalize a title before a name ("CEO Jane Smith"),
  lowercase after a name ("Jane Smith, chief executive officer"). [eReleases, ibid.]
- **Length:** ideal range is 300-500 words, with ~400 words as the most-recommended
  sweet spot (about one single-spaced page) — long enough to tell the story, short
  enough that a journalist scanning 50-100 pitches a day will actually read it.
  73% of journalists prefer press releases under 500 words; releases over 600 words
  signal a lack of understanding of media needs, and 400-600-word releases get
  significantly higher pickup rates than longer ones. [PBJ Stories / PressCable,
  Ideal Press Release Length 2026](https://presscable.com/insights/how-long-should-a-press-release-be-ideal-word-count/)
- **Quotes:** include one or two quotes from key spokespeople; quotes should avoid
  fluff ("we're thrilled to share...") and must add insight or something genuinely new
  rather than restate the lead. Where possible, source quotes from multiple
  stakeholders (e.g., a third party or customer, not only the company) to add
  perspective and authenticity. Attribution: full name + title on first reference,
  last name only on subsequent references. [Wylie Comm / Content Grip, Press Release
  Quote Guide](https://www.contentgrip.com/press-release-quote/)
- **Boilerplate:** placed at the very end, just before media contact information.
  Keep it to 100 words or less (under 50 is better); written in third person (not
  first, which reads as opinionated, or second, which reads as advertising copy);
  should answer whom the company helps, what it makes/does, and where it's located.
  [Wylie Comm, Press Release Boilerplate Best Practices](https://www.wyliecomm.com/press-release-boilerplate-best-practices/)

## 2. B2B vs. B2C

- **Tone:** B2B releases are data-driven, formal, and focused on business-efficiency
  impact, using industry-specific terminology the professional audience already
  understands. B2C releases are more informal, focused on personal benefit,
  lifestyle impact, and emotional appeal, using relatable/conversational language and
  avoiding jargon. [Channel V Media, B2B vs B2C PR](https://channelvmedia.com/blog/difference-between-b2b-b2c-pr/)
- **Audience:** B2B press releases target niche audiences — industry professionals,
  decision-makers, specialists (engineers, project managers) — who want expertise,
  industry-trend context, and operational/profitability-relevant solutions. B2C
  releases target a broad consumer audience interested in lifestyle, entertainment,
  and convenience angles. [Channel V Media, ibid.]
- **Distribution cadence:** B2B media outlets often have longer publishing cycles,
  especially content tied to trade shows or quarterly industry trends, requiring
  outreach planned months ahead of the editorial calendar; B2C PR is generally more
  reactive/timely. [Channel V Media, ibid.]
- **Relationship goal:** B2B PR builds long-term relationships with decision-makers
  through data and industry expertise; B2C PR builds emotional connection through
  storytelling and lifestyle framing. [Channel V Media, ibid.]

## 3. Content Goal Variations

narrower than social formats (the genre itself is largely an announcement/awareness

distinct, extractable claims over generic boilerplate language.

## 4. Funnel Stage Adaptations

- **Press releases sit largely outside the funnel-stage model that applies to social
  content** — their primary job is record-of-announcement and earned/owned media
  reach, not funnel progression. ⚠ KNOWLEDGE-BASED characterization, but supported by
  the goal-variation finding above (no source frames a press release as a MOFU/BOFU
  nurture tool).
- **Distribution-channel realities (2026):** wire services (PR Newswire, Business
  Wire, GlobeNewswire) still distribute to thousands of outlets, cost $400-$1,500+
  per release depending on targeting, and generate backlinks/syndication supporting
  SEO and entity-building. However, Google treats wire-service-distributed links as
  non-editorial and requires `rel="nofollow"` — these links do not pass PageRank and
  do not directly move search rankings on their own. [Sharism.org / StreetInsider,
  Press Release SEO 2026](https://sharism.org/press-release-seo-2026/)
- **SEO/backlink value today is real but has "matured," not died:** the era of
  blasting 500-word releases across low-quality wires for link juice is over, but
  strategic distribution through quality, high-domain-authority outlets still
  delivers measurable brand-entity signals, and multi-site presence that both Google
  and AI systems reward — SEO value now depends on publisher quality, not distribution
  volume. [EasyPRwire / RedPress, Press Release SEO Guide](https://easyprwire.com/blog/press-release-seo-guide)
- **Direct-to-journalist remains a distinct, effective channel:** identifying 20-50
  journalists who cover the relevant beat, sending a personalized email with the
  release pasted directly in the body (not as an attachment) and referencing the
  journalist's recent work — personalization is described as the single biggest
  factor in open rates. [Sharism.org, ibid.]
- **Net finding for "do people still send that stuff?":** yes — press releases in
  2026 serve three functions simultaneously: (1) direct-to-journalist media pickup
  (still real but highly competitive, favoring short/scannable releases per §1), (2)
  SEO/backlink and brand-entity signal generation via quality wire placement (matured,
  not the volume-driven tactic of a decade ago), and (3) an owned/durable
  record-of-announcement even absent any pickup. Treat all three as legitimate goals
  a press release can serve, not just "getting picked up by a journalist."

## 5. Audience Expertise Adaptations

⚠ KNOWLEDGE-BASED — no source directly addressed novice/familiar/expert audience
adaptations for press releases specifically. Directional inference from the B2B/B2C
finding above: releases aimed at trade/specialist audiences can use industry
terminology the audience already knows (B2B pattern); releases aimed at a general
consumer or broad-press audience should define terms and lead with the
plain-language benefit (B2C pattern) rather than assume specialist vocabulary.

## 6. Persuasion Principles

- **Specificity as credibility signal:** quotes must add genuine insight, not
  generic enthusiasm — "we're thrilled to share" is explicitly called out as fluff to
  avoid; a quote that states something specific and new functions as a stronger
  credibility/persuasion device than a generic statement. [Wylie Comm, ibid.] — this
  mirrors the specificity-as-persuasion finding already documented in
  `docs/research/cc-content-facebook-post.md` §6 and `cc-content-instagram-post.md`
  §6.
- **Third-party/social-proof sourcing:** including quotes from customers or other
  third parties (not only company executives) lends "additional perspectives and
  authenticity" — a direct application of the social-proof principle already
  documented in `docs/research/cc-content-x-post.md` §6, applied here via quote
  sourcing rather than engagement metrics. [Content Grip, Press Release Quote Guide,
  ibid.]
- **Authority:** attribution conventions (full name + title on first reference)
  exist specifically to establish the authority of the person being quoted before
  their words are read — a direct, structural application of Cialdini's authority
  principle. ⚠ KNOWLEDGE-BASED synthesis connecting the attribution-format finding to
  the authority principle — not stated directly by the source, which only describes
  the format convention itself.
