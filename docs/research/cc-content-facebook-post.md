# Research: Facebook Posts

Gathered via `WebSearch` on 2026-07-20 for `cc-content-facebook-post`. Covers the six
research areas from `_shared/content-skill-research-brief.md`. Hard numbers are cited
inline; anything without a source is marked `⚠ KNOWLEDGE-BASED`.

## 1. Universal Structure

- **Character limit:** 63,206 characters max; mobile feed truncates the primary text
  after roughly 125-480 characters (sources vary: ~125 chars before "See more" on
  mobile per one source, ~480 chars in a broader feed-truncation figure per another) —
  treat 125 characters as the safer mobile-first truncation point to write a
  self-contained hook before it. [TypeCount](https://typecount.com/blog/facebook-post-character-limit-2026); [AdLibrary, Facebook Ad Copy 2026](https://adlibrary.com/posts/facebook-ad-copy-best-practices)
- **Optimal length for organic reach:** posts of 40-80 characters get 66% higher
  engagement than longer posts; posts under 80 characters earn the highest reach for
  updates/promotions/announcements; 80-150 characters outperforms longer copy when the
  goal is link clicks or comments; posts over 200 characters only perform well when
  clearly structured and value-driven (educational content, storytelling, expert
  advice). [Hook Agency / Letter Counter, Facebook Character Limits 2026](https://lettercounter.org/blog/facebook-character-limit-guide/)
- **Copy length by audience warmth (ad copy research, applicable to organic
  high-consideration posts):** cold/unfamiliar audiences need more copy (150-300 words)
  to build context before asking for a click; warm/already-familiar audiences convert
  better with short, punchy copy (30-80 words) referencing something specific they
  already know. [Deep Marketing, Persuasive Social/Ads Content 2026](https://www.deepmarketing.it/en/blog/persuasive-content-social-ads-2026)
- **Hook / opening:** the first line is the single most important element — it must
  either earn the "See more" click or stand alone as a complete unit if it won't be
  expanded. It should state the problem or promise in the first second; it doesn't
  need to sell, only to earn the next second of attention. [AdLibrary, ibid.]
- **Effective hook patterns:** questions (trigger the brain's pattern-completion
  instinct — the reader starts answering before consciously deciding to engage) and
  problem-agitation (name a specific pain point and intensify it before the
  post/offer's resolution, moving the reader from passive scrolling to active
  problem-solving). [Adligator, Facebook Ad Hook Patterns 2026](https://adligator.com/blog/facebook-ad-hook-patterns-2026)
- **CTA:** one post, one CTA — multiple CTAs in the same post reduce conversion via
  choice overload. Effective CTAs name the action AND set the expectation for what
  follows; direct/specific CTAs ("See pricing") outperform vague/aspirational ones
  ("Unlock your potential"). [AdLibrary, ibid.]
- **Formatting / high-low performer differentiator:** native video gets 2-3x the reach
  of the same video shared as an external link (e.g., YouTube); Facebook's 2026
  algorithm favors content that keeps users on-platform, so any external link
  (including blog links) sees reduced visibility versus a native post. Ranking order
  observed: native video > high-quality photo posts > link posts (moderate, lowest of
  the three). [SocialBee, Facebook Algorithm 2026](https://socialbee.com/blog/facebook-algorithm/)

## 2. B2B vs. B2C

- **Tone:** B2C content is frequently fun and light-hearted (humor, bold visuals, pop
  culture references are "fair game"); B2B adopts a more serious, formal,
  expertise-signaling tone emphasizing clarity, evidence, and reliability — often
  leaning on case studies, performance metrics, and client success stories to justify
  investment. [Ready Artwork](https://www.readyartwork.com/b2b-vs-b2c-marketing-strategies-tactics-and-messaging-compared/); [98BuckSocial](https://98bucksocial.com/understanding-the-difference-between-b2b-and-b2c-marketing/)
- **Depth/length:** B2B content tends to be more detailed (long-form B2B articles
  average ~2,500 words with cited research), while B2C content "cuts to the chase"
  (long-form B2C posts average ~800 words, conversational). ⚠ these long-form figures
  are for articles/blog content, not Facebook posts specifically — apply directionally
  (B2B Facebook posts can run longer/denser than B2C Facebook posts) rather than as
  literal Facebook-post-length targets. [Improvado, B2B vs B2C Content Marketing 2026](https://improvado.io/blog/b2b-b2c-content-marketing-guide)
- **Core goal difference:** B2B content "educates to reassure" (building trust,
  reputation) while B2C content "inspires to drive action" (awareness, sales,
  new-customer acquisition). [Straight North](https://www.straightnorth.com/blog/content-marketing-for-b2b-vs-b2c-companies/)

## 3. Content Goal Variations

⚠ KNOWLEDGE-BASED for a goal-by-goal breakdown specific to Facebook — no source
enumerated tactics per goal (awareness/consideration/conversion/retention/advocacy/
education) for this platform specifically. Reasonable adaptation from findings above:
awareness favors native video/photo + a broad-appeal hook; consideration favors
150-300-word educational or storytelling posts (per the "posts over 200 characters
perform well when clearly structured and value-driven" finding); conversion favors a
short, single-CTA post following the warm-audience 30-80-word guidance; advocacy
leans on Group-style community posts (see funnel-stage note below) since Groups give
equal algorithmic weight to member content.

## 4. Funnel Stage Adaptations

- **Groups vs. Pages as a funnel lever:** Pages reach only 2-6% of followers per post
  organically, while Groups reach 30-60% of members (a 10,000-member Group post
  reaches ~2,000-4,000 members vs. ~200-600 for the same-size Page audience) —
  Facebook's 2026 algorithm treats Groups as a "Community Bridge" and actively
  surfaces member content, giving equal weight to all members' posts rather than
  prioritizing business/influencer content the way Pages do. Practical funnel mapping:
  Pages function as the top-of-funnel "storefront" (broad reach via ads/native video),
  Groups function as the middle/bottom-of-funnel "living room" (community, retention,
  advocacy) — most businesses that use both structure it this way. [PilotPoster](https://www.pilotposter.com/blog/facebook-groups-vs-pages-for-business/); [SocialAppsHQ](https://www.socialappshq.com/facebook/fb-page-vs-group/)
- TOFU/MOFU/BOFU staging beyond the Group/Page distinction is ⚠ KNOWLEDGE-BASED —
  apply the general length/CTA findings above (TOFU: broad hook + video, short; MOFU:
  longer, value-driven, educational; BOFU: short, single, specific CTA).

## 5. Audience Expertise Adaptations

⚠ KNOWLEDGE-BASED — no source directly addressed novice/familiar/expert adaptations
for Facebook specifically. Directional inference from the B2B/B2C depth finding
above: novice-audience posts should stay in the 40-150 character range with plain
language and no jargon; expert-audience posts can run longer (200+ characters) with
denser, evidence-based content per the "well-structured, value-driven" long-post
finding.

## 6. Persuasion Principles

- **Problem-agitation:** naming and intensifying a specific pain point before
  presenting the resolution moves the reader from passive scrolling to active
  problem-solving — directly applicable as a hook technique (see §1). [Adligator, ibid.]
- **Specificity as persuasion:** direct, specific CTAs and claims ("See pricing")
  consistently outperform vague, aspirational ones ("Unlock your potential") —
  specificity itself functions as a credibility/persuasion signal, not just a
  clarity improvement. [AdLibrary, ibid.]
- **Audience-warmth-matched framing (functions like a consistency/commitment
  principle):** warm audiences respond to copy that references something specific
  they already engaged with, leveraging their prior commitment/familiarity rather
  than re-arguing the case from scratch. [Deep Marketing, ibid.]
- Cialdini's broader principles (scarcity, social proof, authority, reciprocity) were
  not re-sourced specifically for Facebook in this pass — apply the same general
  findings already documented in `docs/research/cc-content-x-post.md` §6, since none
  of the sources found here contradicted them; that file cites CXL and Rob Palmer for
  the general mechanics.
