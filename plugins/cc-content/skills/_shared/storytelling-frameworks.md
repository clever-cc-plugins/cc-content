# Storytelling Frameworks — Shared Reference

Shared reference for all content-output skills. Apply a storytelling framework
whenever the output is long enough to carry a narrative structure (roughly 300+
words or ~600+ characters). For shorter outputs — single-paragraph social snippets,
subject lines, taglines — skip this file entirely.

## How to select a framework

1. **Identify the content goal** from the topic or brief (e.g., build trust, sell a
   product, share a lesson, challenge a belief, announce a change).
2. **Note the target audience** if provided — some frameworks suit specific roles
   or contexts better than others.
3. **Find matching frameworks** using the Quick-Selection Guide below. Prefer
   frameworks whose "Best for" aligns with the goal; exclude any listed under
   "Avoid" for this content type.
4. **State the choice** before generating the output:

   > "Using the [Framework Name] structure: [one-sentence reason]."

   If two frameworks are equally strong, pick the simpler one.

## Quick-Selection Guide

| Content goal                                | Recommended framework(s)                                          |
| ------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Promote a product, service, or offer        | AIDA, FAB, Before-After-Bridge, PAS, Star-Chain-Hook              |
| Share a personal insight or lesson learned  | RATTLE, Failure-Learning-Growth, Storytelling Triangle            |
| Build thought leadership / share an opinion | Golden Circle, Expertise Demonstration, Discovery-Insight-Impact  |
| Tell a founder / brand origin story         | Company Origin Story, Personal Origin Story, Rags to Riches       |
| Announce a transformation or career change  | Career Transition Story, Voyage and Return, Rite of Passage       |
| Challenge conventional thinking             | Overcoming Monster, Challenger Sale, Truth-Telling                |
| Inspire or motivate an audience             | Hero's Journey, Heroic Act, Fear-Hope-Action, Redemption Story    |
| Explain a complex concept simply            | Technical Explanation Arc, Pixar's Story, Listicle                |
| Build trust and authentic connection        | Authenticity Framework, Trust Building Narrative, Anti-Hero Story |
| Address conflict or organizational change   | Conflict Resolution Story, Change Story Arc, Strategic Narrative  |
| Drive urgency / call for decision           | Point of No Return, Monster in the House, Tragedy                 |
| Showcase team / partnership success         | Buddy Story, Team Transformation, Stakeholder Journey             |

## Framework Reference (62 frameworks)

### AIDA

**Timeless tool for driving attention and conversion**
Phases: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
Best for: Social media ads, product launches, email campaigns, landing pages
Avoid: Complex B2B sales, long-term loyalty building, personal branding

---

### Anti-Hero Story (Antiheld-Geschichte)

**Human flaws create deeper connection**
Phases: Flaws → Actions → Consequences → Redemption → Ambiguity
Best for: Authentic leadership narratives, moral dilemma stories, personal growth campaigns
Avoid: Inspirational hero stories, children's content, simple success stories

---

### Authenticity Framework

**Strategic authenticity for trust and credibility**
Phases: Challenge → Struggle → Vulnerability → Learning → Growth
Best for: CEO crisis communications, personal brand development, influencer content
Avoid: Standardized product ads, mass communication without emotion, pure fact delivery

---

### Before-After-Bridge

**Show the transformation, then explain how to get there**
Phases: Before → After → Bridge
Best for: Sales pages, product demos, change management, career coaching
Avoid: Complex technical documentation, dry data analysis, pure information brochures

---

### Buddy Story

**Two characters, one synergy**
Phases: Pairing → Conflict → Cooperation → Bonding → Synergy
Best for: Co-founder stories, team building, partnership marketing
Avoid: Solo success stories, solopreneur content, complex family narratives

---

### Career Transition Story

**Navigating identity through professional change**
Phases: Previous Chapter → Realization → Transition Catalyst → Bridge Building → New Identity → Integration → Future Vision
Best for: LinkedIn profiles for career changers, job interviews with career gaps, career coaching content
Avoid: Entry-level positions, technical CVs without narrative, profiles with no career change

---

### Challenger Sale

**Reframe the customer's reality before offering the solution**
Phases: Warmer → Reframe → Rational Drowning → Emotional Impact → Value Proposition → Solution → New Way → Your Solution
Best for: Long-term B2B partnerships, enterprise sales, breaking status-quo thinking
Avoid: Simple product sales, customers with fixed requirements, short-term sales goals

---

### Change Story Arc

**Six steps through organizational transformation**
Phases: Status Quo → Disruption → Resistance → Exploration → Commitment → Integration
Best for: Large reorganizations, digitization projects, culture change initiatives
Avoid: Crisis interventions, one-off campaigns, product launches without systemic change

---

### Character Arc Framework

**The inner transformation that drives the outer story**
Phases: Flaw → Want/Need → Catalyst → Resistance → Breaking Point → Revelation → Choice → Transformation
Best for: Employee coaching stories, personal success narratives, motivation workshops
Avoid: Technical manuals, product descriptions, advertising slogans

---

### Comedy

**Shared laughter creates shared identity**
Phases: Confusion → Misunderstanding → Escalation → Recognition → Joyful Resolution
Best for: Team building, brand personality, social media campaigns with humor
Avoid: Serious crisis communication, legal content, technical manuals

---

### Company Origin Story

**Where you came from explains why you exist**
Phases: Beginnings → Idea → Struggles → Breakthrough → Growth → Legacy
Best for: Employer branding, onboarding, PR campaigns, investor relations
Avoid: Daily social media posts, short-term promotions, product launches without backstory

---

### Conflict Resolution Story

**Depersonalizes conflict, reveals shared ground**
Phases: Tension → Escalation → Perspectives → Common Ground → Resolution
Best for: Team conflict communications, business mediation, stakeholder management
Avoid: Simple decision-making, quick problem-solving, one-sided conflict situations

---

### Discovery-Insight-Impact

**From observation to breakthrough to real-world change**
Phases: Exploration → Discovery → Insight → Application → Impact
Best for: Innovation workshops, research findings, thought leadership development
Avoid: Short advertising slogans, routine communications, quick sales presentations

---

### Emotional Journey Map

**Guide the audience through the full emotional arc of change**
Phases: Status Quo → Disruption → Denial → Anger → Bargaining → Depression → Testing → Acceptance → New Reality
Best for: Therapeutic content, emotional brand experiences, change management
Avoid: Fast factual information delivery, technical documentation, simple product guides

---

### Empathy-Action Bridge

**Turn empathy into effective action**
Phases: Witness → Feel → Understand → Connect → Empower → Act
Best for: NGO donation campaigns, CSR programs, social impact marketing
Avoid: Product-only sales campaigns, tech marketing without emotional component, number-heavy financial content

---

### Expertise Demonstration

**Show, don't just claim, your authority**
Phases: Problem Area → Expertise → Results → Approach
Best for: Thought leadership presentations, consulting services, expert sessions
Avoid: Entertainment content, product advertising, beginner workshops

---

### FAB Formula (Features-Advantages-Benefits)

**Link product attributes directly to customer value**
Phases: Features → Advantages → Benefits
Best for: Technical product descriptions, B2B sales conversations, software presentations
Avoid: Creative emotional storytelling, social media without product context

---

### Failure-Learning-Growth

**Turn setbacks into credibility**
Phases: Confidence → Failure → Impact → Reflection → Learning → Growth
Best for: Startup post-mortems, error culture workshops, resilience training, authentic leadership posts
Avoid: Immediate crisis management, routine process optimization

---

### Fear-Hope-Action

**Transform fear into hope, then direct it**
Phases: Fear → Consequences → Turning Point → Hope → Action
Best for: Health campaigns, climate change awareness, crisis communication
Avoid: Luxury product advertising, comedy content, children's stories

---

### Features-Benefits-Proof (FBP)

**Claims backed by evidence**
Phases: Features → Benefits → Proof
Best for: Technical product presentations, B2B sales documents, complex software descriptions
Avoid: Simple emotional storytelling, entertainment, emotional brand building

---

### Fish-out-of-Water

**Culture shock reveals hidden truths**
Phases: Comfort Zone → New Environment → Culture Shock → Adaptation → New Perspective
Best for: Expat experiences, market entry in new countries, onboarding new employees
Avoid: Technical product demos, financial reporting, routine process documentation

---

### Freytag's Pyramid (Freytag-Pyramide)

**Classical dramatic arc with inevitable resolution**
Phases: Exposition → Rising Action → Climax → Falling Action → Catastrophe
Best for: Tragedies, classical dramas, literary analysis, historical films
Avoid: Complex non-linear narratives, interactive stories, very short content

---

### Golden Circle (Simon Sinek)

**Start with why, not what**
Phases: Why → How → What
Best for: Vision statements, brand purpose, leadership communication, company culture posts
Avoid: Product detail explanations, technical instructions, short-term sales strategies

---

### Heroic Act (Heldenakt)

**One decisive moment defines a character**
Phases: Normalcy → Call to Courage → Decision → Heroic Act → Transformation
Best for: Employee recognition, CSR campaigns with social focus, inspirational keynotes
Avoid: Long-term strategy development, deep character studies, complex multi-stage narratives

---

### Hero's Journey

**The universal story of transformation through challenge**
Phases: Ordinary World → Call to Adventure → Refusal → Mentor → Threshold → Tests → Innermost Cave → Ordeal → Reward → Road Back → Resurrection → Return with Elixir
Best for: Product launches with emotional story, keynotes on personal transformation, brand development
Avoid: Short social media posts, technical documentation, data-heavy reports

---

### Innovation Story

**From problem to prototype to impact**
Phases: Problem → Insight → Idea → Prototype → Impact
Best for: Technology startup pitches, new product innovation presentations, R&D communication
Avoid: Non-innovation communication, traditional business models, narratives without tech progress

---

### Kishōtenketsu

**Harmony through surprise — no conflict required**
Phases: Ki (Setup) → Shō (Development) → Ten (Twist) → Ketsu (Reconciliation)
Best for: Manga-style storytelling, haiku-inspired content, non-confrontational surprise narratives
Avoid: Action-packed thrillers, conflict-centered dramas, political satires

---

### Leadership Journey

**The long arc of a leader's development**
Phases: Calling → Resistance → Mentorship → Trials → Transformation → Wisdom → Return
Best for: Executive coaching content, keynote presentations, leadership autobiographies, mentoring programs
Avoid: Technical training without leadership elements, data-heavy presentations, product-focused marketing

---

### Listicle (Liste/Listicle)

**Numbered takeaways for maximum shareability**
Phases: Choose number → Value promise → Set context → Create points → Add detail → Examples → Transitions → Summary
Best for: Listicle blog posts, highly shareable social media content, infographics, product comparisons
Avoid: Deep scientific analyses, long narrative stories, pure dialogue formats

---

### Monster in the House

**A contained threat that forces action**
Phases: Safe Space → Warning Signs → Monster Appears → Trapped → Fight → Sacrifice → Victory
Best for: Cybersecurity training, crisis prevention, change resistance campaigns
Avoid: Routine projects, standard company introductions, simple product descriptions

---

### Nested Loops

**Stories within stories that deepen engagement**
Phases: Introduction → Deepening → Expansion → Resolution C → Resolution B → Finale
Best for: High-tension keynotes, complex teaching methods, detailed product presentations
Avoid: Short presentations under 5 minutes, simple product descriptions, direct sales pitches

---

### Overcoming Monster (Overcoming Monster)

**Master the threat and emerge stronger**
Phases: Threat → Call → Preparation → Confrontation → Victory
Best for: Disruptive product launches, crisis communication, competitive analysis
Avoid: Long-term partnership strategies, positive brand image campaigns, harmony-focused events

---

### Past-Present-Future Bridge

**Heritage meets vision across a pivotal now**
Phases: Heritage → Reality → Transition → Vision → Bridge
Best for: Company acquisitions, new vision announcements, sustainable business model transitions
Avoid: Fast product launches, simple product advertising, immediate crisis communication

---

### Personal Origin Story

**The roots of who you are explain everything you do**
Phases: Roots → Catalyst → Journey → Struggles → Breakthrough → Mission
Best for: Speaker biographies, LinkedIn About sections, personal branding workshops, networking events
Avoid: Technical product descriptions, financial reports, pure data presentations

---

### Pixar's Story Framework

**Emotionally structured storytelling in eight phases**
Phases: Setup → Routine → Incident → Consequence → Escalation → Climax → Resolution → Lesson
Best for: Animation-style brand storytelling, explainer videos, emotional brand content
Avoid: Data-heavy business reports, technical manuals, long historical documentation

---

### Point of No Return (Punkt ohne Wiederkehr)

**The moment that makes retreat impossible**
Phases: Pressure Builds → Critical Moment → Decision → New Reality → Adaptation
Best for: Strategic business decisions, personal life changes, crisis management scenarios
Avoid: Routine processes, simple information delivery, long-term planning without urgency

---

### Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS)

**Maximize conversion through emotional problem amplification**
Phases: Problem → Agitation → Solution
Best for: Revealing unconscious needs, emotional storytelling in ads, direct sales emails, LinkedIn engagement posts
Avoid: Complex technical explanations, product comparisons without emotion, long-term brand strategies

---

### Process Improvement Story

**Data-driven transformation with measurable results**
Phases: Pain Point → Analysis → Hypothesis → Implementation → Results → Scaling
Best for: Lean management, Six Sigma, Agile improvement, operations excellence content
Avoid: Creative brainstorming, one-off event planning, pure PR campaigns

---

### RATTLE Framework

**Emotionally resonant LinkedIn posts with high engagement**
Phases: Resonance → Accept → Turn → Twist → List → End
Best for: LinkedIn posts, newsletters, blog articles, social media content with CTAs
Avoid: Technical instructions without emotional context, legal texts, scientific reports

---

### Rags to Riches

**From nothing to everything — the archetypal success story**
Phases: Origin → Opportunity → Work → Setbacks → Success
Best for: Founder stories, motivational speeches, inspiring campaigns, success narratives
Avoid: Tragic stories without happy endings, complex political analysis, horror or thriller content

---

### Redemption Story

**The path back from a serious mistake**
Phases: Fall → Consequences → Recognition → Atonement → Redemption
Best for: Crisis communication, personal comeback stories, brand revival campaigns
Avoid: New product introductions, wedding stories, pure success stories

---

### Resurrection/Redemption Story (Auferstehung/Erlösung)

**New life after collapse — hope and renewal**
Phases: Ending → Dark Night → Hope → Rebirth → New Life
Best for: Turnaround stories after company crises, personal crisis recovery, rebranding after reputation damage
Avoid: Long-term growth stories, product launches without backstory, success stories without prior crisis

---

### Rite of Passage (Übergangsritus)

**Identity transformation through a threshold experience**
Phases: Old Identity → Threshold → Liminality → Trials → New Identity
Best for: Onboarding processes, promotions, retirement announcements, major life transitions
Avoid: Short-term projects, everyday tasks, crisis management

---

### Save the Cat

**Make the audience root for the protagonist immediately**
Phases: Opening Image → Setup → Theme → Catalyst → Debate → Break into Two → Fun and Games → Midpoint → Bad Guys Close In → All Is Lost → Dark Night → Finale
Best for: Emotional commercials, character-centered brand films, video content with emotional depth
Avoid: Ultra-short social media clips, simple product demos, documentaries without fictional elements

---

### Stakeholder Journey

**Map and optimize every stakeholder relationship**
Phases: Mapping → Pain Points → Value Prop → Engagement → Success Metrics → Dialogue → Mutual Value
Best for: Public sector negotiations, CSR initiatives, business partnerships
Avoid: Simple internal communications, short-term marketing campaigns, individual customer interactions

---

### Star-Chain-Hook

**Hook first, prove it with a chain, close with commitment**
Phases: Star → Chain → Hook
Best for: YouTube vlogs, Instagram Stories, Twitter/LinkedIn threads, trending topic blog posts
Avoid: Detailed technical articles, technical documentation, long-term brand strategies without flexibility

---

### StoryBrand (Donald Miller)

**The customer is the hero — you are the guide**
Phases: Character → Problem → Guide → Plan → Call to Action → Success/Failure
Best for: Brand messaging, website copy, sales funnel development, customer relationship building
Avoid: Technical documentation, detailed product descriptions, scientific articles

---

### Storytelling Triangle

**Elevator pitches with an emotional core**
Phases: Character → Conflict → Resolution
Best for: Elevator pitches, high-impact social media posts, testimonials, micro-stories
Avoid: Complex novels with deep subplots, detailed technical instructions, multi-layered historical narratives

---

### Strategic Narrative

**Transform strategy into an emotional story**
Phases: Context → Challenge → Vision → Strategy → Action
Best for: Strategy rollout, investor relations, merger communications
Avoid: Small fast-moving projects, daily operational decisions, short-term marketing campaigns

---

### Team Transformation

**The arc from forming to sustained high performance**
Phases: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Transforming → Sustaining
Best for: Project kickoffs, retrospectives, integrating new team members, conflict resolution
Avoid: Individual coaching without team context, established teams without change, short-term projects

---

### Technical Explanation Arc

**Make complex technology understandable**
Phases: Familiar → Bridge → Core → Apply
Best for: API guides, science communication for laypeople, product manuals
Avoid: Creative storytelling, emotional narratives, short fictional pieces

---

### Three-Act Structure

**The foundational dramatic arc of Western storytelling**
Phases: Setup → Inciting Incident → Plot Point 1 → Rising Action → Midpoint → Plot Point 2 → Climax → Falling Action → Resolution
Best for: Film scripts, presentations with convincing argumentation, case studies
Avoid: Very short content under 1,000 words, complex non-linear narratives, interactive stories

---

### Tragedy

**Fatal flaws lead to inevitable downfall — a cautionary tale**
Phases: Noble Start → Fatal Flaw → Warnings Ignored → Downfall → Catastrophe
Best for: Error analysis in safety briefings, ethics training, risk avoidance crisis communication
Avoid: Team motivation, sales kickoffs, success celebrations

---

### Trust Building Narrative

**Systematic trust development through transparency and consistency**
Phases: Transparency → Reliability → Competence → Benevolence → Vulnerability → Consistency → Reciprocity
Best for: Long-term B2B client retention, brand trust building, strategic partnerships
Avoid: Short-term sales strategies, anonymous online transactions, one-sided negotiations

---

### Truth-Telling (Wahrheit sagen)

**Authentic crisis communication through radical honesty**
Phases: Hidden Truth → Pressure Builds → Truth Revealed → Initial Chaos → Liberation
Best for: Whistleblower reports, crisis communication for PR scandals, authentic brand communication
Avoid: Entertainment without deeper message, short-term ad campaigns

---

### Underdog Story (Underdog-Geschichte)

**From outsider to winner against the odds**
Phases: Disadvantage → Dismissal → Determination → Strategic Advantage → Triumph
Best for: Startups vs market leaders, challenger brands, innovative tech vs established players
Avoid: Market leaders without competition, markets without clear hierarchies

---

### Visionary's Path (Weg des Visionärs)

**Master the lonely journey toward innovation**
Phases: Vision → Isolation → Doubt → Perseverance → Validation
Best for: Startup founder stories, disruptive tech leaders, pioneer project narratives
Avoid: Routine business processes, linear growth strategies, short-term profit maximization

---

### Voyage and Return

Avoid: Routine job descriptions, short-term projects, technical reports

### What-So What-Now What

Phases: What → So What → Now What

Avoid: Creative brainstorming sessions, simple information delivery, quick ad-hoc decisions

Avoid: Technical instructions, simple product descriptions, short-term promotions
